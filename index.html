What to do when you get Covid-19?

Stay home as much as possible, and away from other people. If you live with other people, stay in a specific room and wear a mask around others. Get rest, make sure to stay hydrated. Monitor your symptoms and get help from a healthcare professional if your symptoms get worse. If you have any medical appointments, let your medical professional know you have Covid-19.

Quarantine protocols

When you have came into contact with a person that has Covid-19, make sure to stay home and distance yourself from other people for at least 14 days and watch for possible symptoms.

Covid-19 testing

There are a many Covid-19 testing sites available for free. Contact your health care provider or visit your state health department's website to find the lasted details and locations to get a test.

Virus information

The Coronavirus, also known as Covid-19 is an infectious disease that originated in China. THis virus spreads through respiratory droplets of saliva or discharge from the nose and mouth when a person sneezes or coughs. People who are older ages or have underlying medical conditions are more likely to contract Covid-19. Most people with the virus experience mild symptoms and can recover without medica treatment.

Symptoms may include:

  • fever or chills
  • shortness of breath
  • nausea or vomitting
  • headache
  • body aches
  • cough
  • sore throat
  • runny nose or congestion
  • loss of smell or taste
