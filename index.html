What to do when you get Covid-19?

Stay home as much as possible, and away from other people. If you live with other people, stay in a specific room and wear a mask around others. Get rest, make sure to stay hydrated. Monitor your symptoms and get help from a healthcare professional if your symptoms get worse. If you have any medical appointments, let your medical professional know you have COVID-19.

Quarantine Protocols:

When you have came into contact with a person that has COVID-19, make sure to stay home and distance yourself from other people for at least 14 days and watch for possible symptoms. Make sure to wear a mask, and take care of your health by getting enough rest and drinking enough water.

COVID-19 Testing:

Coronavirus testing helps determine if a person has the virus. There are a many COVID-19 testing sites available for free. Contact your health care provider or visit your state health department's website to find the latest details and locations to get a test.

Vaccination Information:

The Coronavirus can be prevented with a vaccine. Vaccines teach the immune system to recognize and fight COVID-19. Sometimes a person may experience symptoms after getting their shot, but this is completely normal and is a sign that the body is building protection against the virus. Vaccines have been professionally developed and are required to meet many safety requirements before they are distributed for public use. They are safe, and cannot infect people with the virus. There are also many vaccination centers available, visit your state health department's website to find more information.

Virus Information:

The Coronavirus, also known as COVID-19 is an infectious disease that originated in China. This virus spreads through respiratory droplets of saliva or discharge from the nose and mouth when a person sneezes or coughs. People who are older ages or have underlying medical conditions are more likely to contract COVID-19. Most people with the virus experience mild symptoms and can recover without medical treatment.

Symptoms May Include:

  • Fever or Chills
  • Shortness of Breath
  • Nausea or Vomiting
  • Headache
  • Body Aches
  • Cough
  • Sore Throat
  • Runny Nose or Congestion
  • Loss of Smell or Taste
